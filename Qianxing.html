Qianxing Rural Public Welfare

This is a student-initiated, non-profit website. Our mission is to connect charitable volunteer groups involved in rural areas, and to help people gain a better understanding of rural charity. On this website, users can initiate a new rural public welfare activity or join an activity that they are interested in. Moreover, users can publish articles and pictures about their activity and team leaders can also post important notices or share documents such as meeting records between team members.

We hope this website will encourage more people to take action and help people in rural areas.

THE DEMANDS
At the beginning, we determined our target users. Target users mainly consist of the volunteer organizations who want to help. These organizations include university associations such as the Loving Heart Society of Peking University and other enterprises.
Then, in order to find out the needs of these users, we carried out several investigations including online surveys and interviews. We summarized those responses into three main points.
  • Huge demand for volunteers in rural areas.
  • City volunteers want to know how they can help the countryside.
  • Volunteers wish to share their experiences and record their own feelings and thoughts.
3
THE VISION
Qianxing will be a social networking platform for rural charity volunteers to exchange information:
  • Charity updates posting: to share experiences with others.
  • Social networking interaction: to interact and communicate with others.
  • Interest-based choice: to choose the event that suits you best.
Qianxing will be an online learning platform for charitable volunteers to learn more about rural charity:
  • Presenting rural demands: to provide the charitable groups with information on what rural areas need.
  • Involvement in editing: to compile an encyclopedia of information for rural charity work.
  • Open online courses: to equip users with knowledge regarding rural charity.
Considering the time we were able to put in, we decided to focus on the social networking platform in the first version of our website. After that, we will add more features like online courses to the website.

OUR WEBSITE
(1) Homepage
This is a webpage that gives visitors the first impression about what we do and what they can do with our website. This page is responsible for showing visitors a simple guideline on how to use our website and more importantly, display the latest information for rural public welfare activities.
Homepage 1.0
Homepage 2.0
The main difference between Homepage 1.0 and Homepage 2.0 is the presentation of activities. In Homepage 2.0, I adopted a "waterfall" layout for the following reasons.
First, visitors are more likely to be attracted to images and the "waterfall" layout can provide space for more pictures. Secondly, this kind of layout enables visitors to gain information more effectively and also it is easy for visitors to view more information by simply scrolling down. Thirdly, a "waterfall" layout will space out all the activities' information elegantly, and by presenting only the essential information of each activity, visitors may focus more on the main content instead of secondary Info.
(2) Activity Page
Activity Page is the webpage that presents details of a rural public welfare activity, including basic information of an activity such as time, place, recruitment deadline, notices and team members. Also, it contains articles and short texts that are relevant to the activity. Visitors can browse this page to get to know about an activity and team members are able to manage their documents and share stories on this webpage.
Activity Page 1.0
Activity Page 2.0
After several debates about Activity Page 1.0, we decided to add a new function to this page, that is, classification of articles and short texts. We reached a consensus that each team has different needs. For example, one team may need to have a special space to store meeting records, while the other may wish to combine their diaries together, so that it would be more reasonable for them to manage their own activity files on this webpage.
Thus, in Activity Page 2.0, I took the new function into account and redesigned the page. First, I rearranged the layout for team cover, team information, team members and other modules to maximize the use of space. I also added a space for pictures and latest Info above the fold, so that readers can view most of information at the first glance without scrolling down. Secondly, I divided the page into two parts to present articles, short texts, milestones and so on. Articles and short texts take up most of the space since they are the main content and they can be classified into different groups such as resource center, meeting records and team journal. Moreover, team leaders can create a new category if they want.
(3) Social Page
This is a webpage that provides a platform for all kinds of users to communicate. Users can post articles or short texts and photos of different rural public welfare activities on this page. They can also make comments or reply under other users' articles or short texts.
Social Page 1.0
Social Page 2.0
The main improvement I made on this webpage is changing the color and layout of the username.
First, I made the "plus" button above the input box invisible and by simply clicking on the text, the user is able to add another activity. This is because the function is not commonly used (normally users post articles or short texts on only one activity). Secondly, I made a change of the background color of the "sent" button and made it more eye-catching to encourage users to post their thoughts. Thirdly, I changed the color of icons for "thanks", "comments" and "forwards" to make the overall style more consistent. Finally, I switched the position of the username from the bottom of the user's avatar to the right to make the username easier to identify and so that it is in line with user habits.
